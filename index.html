Valentin Liévin, PhD

Machine learning researcher at raffle.ai

My main interest lies in large language models and tool augmentations (mostly search). I have recently submitted my PhD thesis Deep Latent Variable Models for Natural Language Processing and graduated from the Technical University of Denmark (DTU).

My recent research concentrated on large language models (LLMs), medical question answering and information retrieval. In "Can large language models reason about medical questions?", I have demonstrated that LLMs like OpenAI's Codex GPT-3 could pass the USMLE exam. I have since shifted my focus to augmenting language models with search components. To learn more about this topic, I invite you to explore my latest paper, "Variatonal Open-Domain Question Answering", ICML 2023.

I am currently working at raffle.ai and building VodLM/vod, an open source project targeted at training retrieval augmented language models at scale.
